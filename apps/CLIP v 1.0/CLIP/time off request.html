Time Off Request Submission Form

Please review the following guidelines carefully before submitting your request:
  1. Advance Notice: Submit your request at least two weeks in advance to ensure timely processing and approval.
  2. Approval Process: All requests require approval from your manager or supervisor. Longer or strategically timed absences may need additional levels of approval.
  3. Blackout Dates: Be aware of any blackout dates when time off is restricted due to peak business periods or important company events.
  4. Leave Balance: Ensure you have sufficient leave balance available by checking your eHub account. The system will not automatically check a your current balance, and an insufficent balance may result in a denial of your leave.
  5. Form Completeness: Complete all required fields in the form, including dates, type of leave, reason for the request, and any necessary documentation. Incomplete forms will automatically be denied.
  6. Documentation: Report supporting documentation such as a doctor’s note for medical leave directly to human resources. Do not give personal resords or information to leadership or management.
  7. Conflict Management: Your request will be reviewed to avoid conflicts with other employees’ time off, ensuring smooth business operations. That being said, we work from a "first come, first served" basis.
  8. Submission Method: Submit your request through this digital platform as it is the designated method for processing time off requests. Forms submitted outside of the platform wil not be considered for approval.
  9. Acknowledgment and Tracking: You will receive an acknowledgment upon submission within the platform. An email will be sent to you with the decision regarding your leave and will act as a receipt of approval.
  10. Emergency Leave: For emergency leave requests, follow the specific guidelines which may allow for shorter notice periods.
  11. Return to Work: Follow the specified process for notifying your manager or HR department upon your return to work.
  12. Communication: Coordinate with your team and manager about your planned absence to ensure all necessary work coverage is arranged.
